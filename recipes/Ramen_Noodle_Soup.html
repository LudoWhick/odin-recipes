Ramen Noodle Soup

Description

Ramen Noodle Soup is a popular Japanese dish that consists of wheat noodles in a flavorful broth. It is typically garnished with various toppings such as sliced pork, green onions, and boiled eggs. The broth can be either soy-based or miso-based, and it is known for its rich and savory taste. Ramen Noodle Soup is a comforting and filling meal that is enjoyed by people of all ages. It is a staple in Japanese cuisine and has gained popularity worldwide. Whether it is a quick lunch or a late-night snack, Ramen Noodle Soup is a delicious and satisfying choice.

Ingredients

  • 3 ½ cups vegetable broth
  • 1 (3.5 ounce) package ramen noodles with dried vegetables
  • 2 teaspoons soy sauce
  • ½ teaspoon chili oil
  • ½ teaspoon minced fresh ginger root
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 2 green onions, sliced

Steps

  1. In a medium saucepan combine broth and noodles.
  2. Cover and bring to a boil over high heat; stir to break up noodles
  3. Reduce heat to medium and add soy sauce, chili oil and ginger.
  4. Simmer, uncovered, for 10 minutes. Stir in sesame oil and garnish with green onions.

Miso Noodle Soup

Description

Miso Noodle Soup is a traditional Japanese dish made with miso paste and noodles. It is a warm and comforting soup that is packed with flavor. The soup is typically made with a broth made from soybeans, rice, barley or other grains, and is mixed with miso paste, which is made from fermented soybeans. The noodles used can vary, but are usually udon or soba noodles. The soup can be customized by adding various toppings such as tofu, mushrooms, seaweed, or green onions. Miso Noodle Soup is a delicious and satisfying dish that is enjoyed by many.

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 teaspoons finely grated fresh ginger
  • 2 teaspoons finely grated garlic
  • ⅓ cup thinly sliced leek (green part)
  • 1 ½ cups thinly sliced cabbage
  • ½ cup enoki mushrooms
  • 4 ounces refrigerated fresh ramen noodles
  • 3 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons miso paste
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce, or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon black sesame seeds, or to taste
  • 2 tablespoons sliced green onion, or to taste

Steps

  1. Crack eggs into a nonstick skillet over medium heat. Cook until outer edges become opaque, about 1 minute. Turn once; cook until whites are completely set, about 3 minutes; set eggs aside.
  2. Heat sesame oil in a saucepan over medium heat; cook and stir ginger, garlic, leek, cabbage, and mushrooms until fragrant, about 1 minute. Stir in miso paste; gradually whisk in water and bring to a boil. Add in ramen noodles; reduce heat and simmer until noodles soften, about 3 minutes. Season with soy sauce. Divide into two bowls; top each serving with egg. Garnish with sesame seeds and scallions.

Miso Beef Noodles

Description

Miso Beef Noodles is a delicious and hearty dish that combines tender beef, flavorful miso broth, and chewy noodles. The dish usually includes thinly sliced beef, vegetables such as bok choy or mushrooms, and a savory miso-based broth. The noodles are cooked separately and then added to the broth just before serving. The combination of umami-rich miso and tender beef creates a comforting and satisfying meal. Miso Beef Noodles are popular in Asian cuisine and can be enjoyed at restaurants or made at home. It's a perfect choice for noodle lovers looking for a flavorful and filling dish.

Ingredients

For the miso beef sauce:

  • 1/3 cup red miso paste
  • 3 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha hot sauce, or to taste
  • 2 1/4 pounds boneless beef chuck roast
  • 1 medium yellow onion, sliced
  • 3 clove cloves garlic, minced
  • 2/3 cups water

For the noodle dish:

  • 3 cups water
  • 1 cup sliced red bell peppers
  • 1/2 cup sliced green onions, plus more to garnish the top
  • 3 cups sliced bok choy
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch, optional
  • 2 teaspoons water to mix with cornstarch
  • 8 ounces bucatini (or any other noodle or pasta)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Combine red miso paste, maple syrup, rice vinegar, soy sauce, garlic powder, black pepper, kosher salt, and sriracha in a mixing bowl and whisk until smooth.
  3. Add beef to the bowl and coat thoroughly on both sides, poking beef all over with a fork to allow the marinade to soak into the meat.
  4. Add onions and minced garlic to a braising pan, and distribute evenly. Top with beef, and all the sauce mixture. Spread sauce over top of beef, and add 2/3 cup water around the meat.
  5. Transfer pan to the preheated oven and roast for 1 hour. Remove from oven, flip beef over, and spoon pan drippings over the top. Return to the oven and roast for 1 hour more. Cover pan and continue roasting for 1 more hour.
  6. Remove from the oven and let cool. Spoon the caramelized onions over the meat. Cover and refrigerate overnight (NOTE: meat can be cut up and the recipe finished as shown without the overnight refrigeration step).
  7. The next day, cut cold beef into 1/2 inch pieces, and return to the pan. Add 3 cups of water, place over high heat, and bring to a simmer. Simmer, stirring occasionally, until the meat is tender, about 45 minutes.
  8. Stir bell peppers, green onions, and bok choy into the pan. Raise heat to medium, and cook until vegetables are as tender as desired, 5 to 10 minutes.
  9. Meanwhile, bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook bucatini in the boiling water, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite, 9 to 12 minutes.
  10. Mix cornstarch and 2 teaspoons of water in a small bowl. Stir into the sauce (only if you like a thicker sauce) and cook for about 1 minutes, while stirring. Transfer cooked noodles to the pan using tongs and toss to combine.
  11. Serve immediately with cilantro and green onions if desired.
